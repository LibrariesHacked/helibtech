Artificial intelligence (AI) is forcing libraries to confront two long-standing challenges. The first is how subject and instruction librarians curate and recommend resources. The second is how those resources are actually delivered into the workflows where students, researchers, and faculty already do their work. AI gives us the ability to understand context and urgency in ways that were not previously possible. It allows us to move from generic curation to contextual guidance. From resources waiting to be found, to expertise delivered when it matters. From static guides to living, responsive support that can stay current as academic needs evolve.
